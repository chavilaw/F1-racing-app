RACETRACK DESIGN STUFF
======================

Just some quick ideas for your project - no need to overthink it!

Quick Start (1 hour total):
1. Check out F1 website - copy their colors
2. Use Bootstrap - just copy/paste their examples
3. Test on your phone - make sure it works

Easy Tools:
- Bootstrap: https://getbootstrap.com/ (just copy their examples)
- Google Fonts: https://fonts.google.com/ (pick Roboto or Open Sans)
- Colors: Just use #DC3545 (red) and #007BFF (blue)

Good Examples to Copy:
- F1 website: https://www.formula1.com/ (copy their red/blue colors)

- Stadium displays on YouTube (copy big text, high contrast)

MOBILE/TABLET INTERFACE EXAMPLES
===============================

1. **Sports Apps**
   - ESPN, BBC Sport, official team apps
   - Features: Touch-friendly, quick access, real-time updates
   - Design elements: Large buttons, clear navigation

2. **Control Panel Apps**
   - Smart home apps, industrial control apps
   - Features: Touch controls, status indicators, quick actions
   - Design elements: Large touch targets, clear feedback

3. **Gaming Mobile Interfaces**
   - Mobile racing games, sports games
   - Features: Touch controls, real-time feedback, clear visuals
   - Design elements: Responsive, touch-optimized, engaging

TYPOGRAPHY INSPIRATION
======================

1. **Racing/Motorsport Fonts**
   - Futura, Avenir, Helvetica Neue
   - Characteristics: Modern, clean, highly readable
   - Use for: Headers, large displays, important information

2. **Dashboard Fonts**
   - Inter, Roboto, Open Sans
   - Characteristics: Professional, readable, versatile
   - Use for: Body text, controls, secondary information

3. **Display Fonts**
   - Impact, Arial Black, Montserrat
   - Characteristics: Bold, attention-grabbing, large sizes
   - Use for: Public displays, countdown timers, flags

IMPLEMENTATION TIPS
===================

1. **Start with Wireframes**
   - Use tools like Figma, Sketch, or even paper
   - Focus on layout and information hierarchy first
   - Don't worry about colors and styling initially

2. **Use Design Systems**
   - Look at: Material Design, Ant Design, Bootstrap
   - Provides: Consistent components, color palettes, typography
   - Saves time and ensures consistency

3. **Test on Real Devices**
   - Test on actual tablets, phones, large displays
   - Check: Touch targets, readability, performance
   - Adjust: Sizes, colors, layouts based on testing

4. **Iterate and Improve**
   - Start with basic functionality
   - Add visual polish gradually
   - Test with real users when possible

NEXT STEPS
==========

2. **Create a mood board** with your favorite examples
